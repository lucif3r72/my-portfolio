WORK / AESM TAP

AESM TAP

Talent Acquisition Portal

Digitised Anglo-Eastern's full talent pipeline: candidate discovery, approval routing, and conditional digital forms, across web and mobile, for a shipping group managing 80,000+ seafarers globally.

UX Research UI Design Design System Prototyping Dev Handoff
CLIENT Anglo-Eastern
ROLE UI/UX Designer
PLATFORM Web + Mobile
STATUS Internal Platform
YEAR 2025
Overview Research Process Design Results Reflection
01 · OVERVIEW

The Problem

Anglo-Eastern's recruitment ran on 10+ paper forms and disconnected systems. HR teams had no efficient way to search vast candidate databases, and approval hierarchies lived in email threads.

The goal was to digitise the entire workflow: intelligent search, RBAC-based approvals, and seamless form completion, without breaking from the established design language.

My Contribution
End-to-End UX Information Architecture Design System Prototyping Dev Handoff Stakeholder Presentations
02 · APPROACH

How We Solved It

The web platform centres on an advanced search architecture with maritime-specific filters for precise candidate discovery. Interfaces adapt dynamically based on RBAC permissions, with smart approval routing replacing email chains.

Paper forms became progressive, contextual digital experiences. The mobile app integrates an AI conversational interview agent via phone, both platforms sharing the same visual language throughout.

Key Research Findings
01

Duplicate Data Entry

HR teams re-entered the same candidate data across three disconnected platforms. No single source of truth existed for the full hiring record.

02

Invisible Approvals

Decisions lived entirely in email threads. No audit trail, no accountability, and no visibility for the candidate or the approving manager.

03

Search That Fails Expertise

Senior seafarers with 15+ years of niche certifications were filtered out by keyword search. The system had no concept of maritime rank or vessel hierarchy.

KEY DESIGN DECISIONS
Search Architecture

Maritime-Specific Filters

Built filter taxonomies around vessel types, rank hierarchies, and certification matrices, replacing generic keyword search with structured maritime logic that surfaces the right candidate every time.

Permissions

RBAC-Driven Interface

Interfaces adapt to the logged-in role: recruiters see candidate pools, managers see approval queues, admins see the full audit trail. No single user is overwhelmed by what is not theirs to action.

Mobile · AI

Conversational Interview Agent

A phone-based AI handles initial candidate screening, feeding structured data back into the platform automatically, eliminating the manual transcription step that previously took hours per candidate.

Forms

Progressive Digitisation

Ten paper forms rebuilt as contextual, conditional digital experiences, each showing only what the current role needs at the current stage, cutting form completion time and reducing input errors.

03 · RESULTS

Outcome

The platform eliminated paper-based delays across the full hiring cycle. Advanced maritime search filters now let HR teams navigate large candidate pools in seconds. RBAC ensures secure, role-appropriate access while automating approval routing.

04 · REFLECTION

What I Learned

Enterprise UX at this scale taught me that the most impactful design decisions are organisational, not visual. Mapping RBAC permission hierarchies before touching a wireframe saved weeks of rework. The approval model had to be fully understood before any screen could be designed responsibly.

If I were starting over, I would push harder for a unified onboarding flow between the web platform and mobile app. The two currently share a visual language but diverge in interaction patterns, a tension worth resolving in Phase 4, and a reminder that design system parity is a product strategy problem, not just a component problem.

This project is ongoing. Findings and design decisions documented here reflect Phases 1–2.
